I feel like a product of our particular zeitgeist: grew up before and after the internet, worked remotely before the pandemic made it normal, built a non-linear career and found it more exciting than a straight line would have been. My current hyperfocus is figuring out how to use AI as an amplifier without letting it replace the things only humans bring. My ceramics and sewing classes are the same instinct in physical form: stay close to making things with your hands. FrameRoom started because my best friend moved into a new place and we wanted to hang posters. I wanted to be the human in the loop on the creative decisions — and to use AI to see a project come to life in days. That tension became the product principle.
