Fresh out of a business degree with no technical background, I was drawn to tech writing because it allowed me to understand how technology worked. What I didn't expect was that not knowing things would turn out to be the job skill. The best technical documentation starts where the reader starts: at the beginning, with honest questions. I happened to be genuinely at the beginning, which meant I asked engineers things no one else thought to ask (bless them for the patience), and wrote docs that actually answered them. From my first internship in tech to actually leading a team of tech writers, four years of working with documentation built something most PMMs don't have: a working mental model of how products are built, not just sold.
