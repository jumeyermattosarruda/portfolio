I didn't switch to PMM because I was tired of tech writing. I switched because I missed adjectives. I missed caring not just about how a product worked, but about why someone should choose it. I'd spent four years being ruthlessly precise — cutting anything argumentative from docs, marking persuasive language red in VScode diffs — and I was good at it. But the creative, business-facing part of my brain was getting restless. When I mapped the decision as a Venn diagram: what skills do I already have, what do I want to learn next, what roles sit close to revenue — product marketing landed in the center every time. My technical fluency would make me a better PMM. And PMM would give me back the "why."
